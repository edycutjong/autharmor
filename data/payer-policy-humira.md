# BlueCross BlueShield — Humira (Adalimumab) Prior Authorization Policy

**Policy Number**: RX-2024-BIO-041  
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2024

**Category**: Specialty Pharmacy — Biologic DMARDs

## Covered Indications

Humira (adalimumab) is covered for the following FDA-approved indications:
- Rheumatoid Arthritis (moderate to severe)
- Psoriatic Arthritis
- Ankylosing Spondylitis
- Crohn's Disease (moderate to severe)
- Ulcerative Colitis (moderate to severe)
- Plaque Psoriasis (moderate to severe)

---

## Prior Authorization Criteria for Rheumatoid Arthritis

### Required Documentation

1. **Diagnosis Confirmation**
   - Confirmed diagnosis of moderate-to-severe rheumatoid arthritis
   - Documentation of disease activity (e.g., DAS28 score, joint counts, inflammatory markers)

2. **Step Therapy Requirements**
   - Patient must have tried and failed **at least ONE conventional DMARD** for a minimum of **3 months** at adequate dosage
   - Acceptable conventional DMARDs include:
     - Methotrexate (≥15mg/week for ≥12 weeks)
     - Leflunomide (20mg/day for ≥12 weeks)
     - Sulfasalazine (2g/day for ≥12 weeks)
     - Hydroxychloroquine (400mg/day for ≥12 weeks)
   
3. **Documentation of Failure or Intolerance**
   - "Failure" is defined as:
     - Inadequate clinical response despite adequate trial duration (≥12 weeks)
     - DAS28 score remaining ≥3.2 after adequate trial
   - "Intolerance" is defined as:
     - Documented adverse event requiring discontinuation
     - Laboratory abnormality (e.g., hepatotoxicity, cytopenias) requiring discontinuation

4. **Prescriber Requirements**
   - Must be prescribed by or in consultation with a rheumatologist

### Important Notes

- **ONE conventional DMARD trial is sufficient** if the patient experienced:
  - Serious adverse event (Grade 3+)
  - Hepatotoxicity (ALT/AST >3x ULN)
  - Severe cytopenias
  - Documented intolerance requiring hospitalization
  
- Prior authorization is valid for **12 months** and may be renewed with documentation of continued medical necessity and clinical response.

---

## Appeals Process

If prior authorization is denied, the prescriber may file an appeal with:
1. Updated clinical documentation
2. Specific citations to the patient's medical record
3. Letter of medical necessity from the treating rheumatologist
4. Relevant clinical guideline references (e.g., ACR 2021 RA Treatment Guidelines)

Appeals must be filed within **180 days** of the denial decision.

---

## Clinical Guideline References

- American College of Rheumatology (ACR) 2021: Recommends biologic DMARDs after failure of conventional DMARD therapy
- EULAR 2022: Supports early biologic use in patients with poor prognostic factors
- FDA Label: Humira is approved for moderate-to-severe RA in adults with inadequate response to DMARDs
